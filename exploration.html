Space Exploration

Timeline

Space exploration is the use of astronomical and space science to explore and study outer space.
Physical study of space can be carried out by manned or robotic spacecraft.

1957

November 3

The Soviet Union invented a satellite called "Sputnik 2"
Ready to send a dog named "Laika" with the satellite
Which is the first living thing in the world to go into space for the first time

1959

January 2

The Soviet Union created the satellite "Luna 1"
and launched it into space to crash into the surface of the moon
and bring back data of the lunar surface to Earth, but failed
because the satellite Luna 1 slipped away and orbited the sun instead.

September 14

After Luna 1 The Soviets failed to invent "Luna 2"
and launched it into space again, this time successfully hitting the
surface of the moon and sending data back to Earth.
It was the first time in the world that a satellite reached the surface of a planet other than Earth.

1961

April 12

The Soviet Union created the "Vostok 1" spacecraft into orbit.
Along with sending "Yuri Gagarin" up with the Vostok 1 spacecraft.
Yuri Gagarin was thus the first cosmonaut in the world to go outside the world.

1964

The United States launched a space probe called "Mariner 4"
Went to explore Mars and took pictures of the Martian surface back to Earth.
It was discovered that Mars has many impact craters, just like the Moon.
And found many volcanic craters.

1969

July 20

The United States has launched a spacecraft "Apollo 11"
Along with the world's first astronauts who stepped on the surface of the moon.

1971

The Soviet Union sent the space probe "Mars 3"
to land on Mars to analyze the surface conditions.

And the United States sent the "Mariner 9" to explore Mars again.
Found volcanoes, ice at the poles and detected 2 moons of Mars.
Which were named Later, "Phobos" and "Deimos".

1975

The United States has launched the "Viking 1" spacecraft. Go to Mars with the "Viking 2" spacecraft to follow
To explore the surface of the valley, traces of life on Mars

1986

February

The Soviet Union successfully sent a space station called "Mir" into orbit around the Earth.
